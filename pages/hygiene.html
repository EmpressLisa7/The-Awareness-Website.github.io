HYGIENE

These are problems for hygiene, their solutions and preventions.

1. Poor Sanitation.

(i) Explanation.

Poor Sanitation is when people who live in a distinct setting don't have access to safe water, a sound sewage system, and a dirty environment. More importantly, it lacks access to proper toilet facilities.

(ii) Solutions.

Creating demand for sanitation services. Promoting good sanitation behaviors. Generating access to latrines and sanitation services. Institutionalizing good governance, management and maintenance of these systems.

(iii) Preventions

The text emphasizes the importance of improving access to clean water, promoting proper waste management, enhancing sanitation facilities, educating the community, supporting health policies, encouraging community involvement, and investing in infrastructure development to ensure clean water, proper waste management, and hygiene practices.

2. Food Poisoning.

(i) Explanation.

Food poisoning, also called foodborne illness, is an infection or irritation of your digestive tract that spreads through food or drinks. Viruses, bacteria, and parasites cause most food poisoning. Harmful chemicals may also cause food poisoning.

(ii) Solutions.

To ensure food safety, follow these steps: wash hands, cook food thoroughly, store food properly, check expiration dates, educate on food safety practices, encourage regular inspections, and promote awareness of foodborne illnesses. Regular inspections and compliance with safety regulations are crucial for maintaining food safety.

(iii) Preventions

To ensure food safety, practice good hygiene, separate raw and cooked foods, cook food to appropriate internal temperatures, store food safely, monitor expiration dates, educate food handlers, and conduct regular inspections. Regular health inspections and reporting of unsafe practices can help prevent cross-contamination and ensure food safety.
